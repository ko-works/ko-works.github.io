Summer programs — the best place to start

We highlight summer programs at Kudan Japanese Language School because that's when many U.S. high school and college students can travel—and it's a great introduction to studying in Japan.

Choosing a language school abroad can feel overwhelming—course types, dates, housing, and paperwork all at once. We're here in the U.S. to answer questions clearly and help you stay organized.

Why Kudan

Kudan offers Japanese language education in Tokyo with a range of programs and activities. Course details, dates, and fees change by year; the school's official site is the source of truth for the latest brochures and application steps.

Why work with KO WORKS

Many students contact schools directly—and that can work. If you'd rather have a U.S.-based, English-speaking contact on the East Coast who understands both sides, we're here to:

  • Answer questions in plain English, in a time zone closer to yours
  • Help you compare options and stay on top of deadlines and paperwork
  • Be a steady point of contact while you coordinate with the school

Other programs at Kudan

Kudan also offers short-term courses (from about two weeks), long-term study (up to about two years, student visa may be required), and specialty options. We don't list every track here—requirements and availability vary.

If summer isn't the right fit, or you're considering a longer stay, reach out. We'll help you figure out next steps.
