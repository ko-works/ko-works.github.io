Why it matters now

Under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), websites are increasingly treated as places of public accommodation. Most websites are not yet accessible — and plaintiffs are paying attention.

3,000+

website accessibility lawsuits filed in U.S. federal courts in 2025 — up sharply from the year before.

~95%

of the top one million website home pages have detectable WCAG failures.

~70%

of these lawsuits target e-commerce websites — often small and mid-sized businesses.

Sources: Seyfarth Shaw ADA Title III Report (2025); WebAIM Million Report (2025).

How we help

WCAG Audit

We review your website against WCAG 2.1 / 2.2 Level AA criteria — the standards referenced by the ADA and most accessibility regulations. You receive a detailed report with every issue identified and prioritized.

Remediation Guidance

We provide clear, actionable fixes for each issue — written so your development team (or ours) can implement them. No vague recommendations; every fix includes the what, where, and how.

Compliance Monitoring

Accessibility isn't a one-time fix. We offer ongoing monitoring to catch new issues as your site evolves — so you stay compliant as content and features change.
